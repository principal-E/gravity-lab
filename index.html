Core Concept

What is gravitational force?

Gravity is a force that pulls objects toward a planet. Think of it like Star Wars — imagine the planets are fighters in a challenge. Jupiter vs Saturn. Jupiter wins every time because it has more mass. The bigger the mass, the stronger the gravity. That is exactly what this project proved.

The formula this app uses

Weight = (Earth Weight ÷ 9.8) × Planet's g

Dividing by 9.8 converts your weight into mass — how much stuff your body is made of. Multiplying by the planet's gravity tells you how hard that planet pulls on you. This is also why rockets cannot carry too much mass — more mass means gravity pulls harder making it harder to launch.

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

F = G × (m₁ × m₂) ÷ r²

Gravity gets weaker the farther apart two objects are. It is like standing next to a speaker — the closer you are the more you feel the vibration. Walk away and you feel less. Gravity works exactly the same way. Newton proved this in 1687 and NASA still uses this formula today.

Saturn Surprise

Why size does not equal gravity

Saturn is huge but its gravity is close to Earth's. Why? Because Saturn is made mostly of gas — basically like air. Size alone does not determine gravity. What really matters is MASS — how much actual stuff the planet is made of.
